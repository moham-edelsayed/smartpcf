Project Booklet

Explore the full technical documentation, architecture, and clinical outcomes of the Smart IoT-Based Healthcare System designed for early disease detection using Plasmonic PCF Sensors.

Format: PDF
25 Pages
Version 1.0
Updated 2025

Abstract

An overarching summary of the integration between highly sensitive Plasmonic PCF sensors and robust IoT cloud architectures to facilitate non-invasive diagnostics.

Objectives

Detailed goals focusing on achieving high sensitivity, lowering latency in IoT data transmission, and providing a scalable solution for modern hospitals.

Methodology

In-depth look at the sensor's cross-sectional design, fabrication constraints, and the DevOps pipeline driving the cloud processing unit.

Results

Analysis of the generated mode fields, confinement loss metrics, and the speed of the IoT gateway response under heavy loads.

Applications

Real-world implementation scenarios in ICUs, remote patient monitoring, and early viral outbreak tracking.

References

Comprehensive citations of related biomedical optics research, IoT standards, and previous literature on PCF technologies.
