Abstract

We propose a modified Photonic Crystal Fiber–Surface Plasmon Resonance (PCF-SPR) biosensor for highly sensitive refractive index sensing and biomedical applications.

  • Redesigned conventional Dual-Side Polished PCF into a circular structure for enhanced light confinement.
  • Utilizes Gold (Au) as the plasmonic material for surface plasmon waves.
  • Numerically analyzed using Finite Element Method (FEM) via COMSOL Multiphysics.
  • Optimized using AI and Machine Learning (Genetic Algorithms).
  • Achieved a maximum wavelength sensitivity of 8000 nm/RIU and resolution of 1.9 × 10⁻⁵ RIU.

Project Objectives

  • Design a high-performance PCF-SPR biosensor.
  • Analyze optical characteristics using FEM (COMSOL).
  • Investigate the effects of air-hole diameter, pitch, and gold layer thickness.
  • Optimize design using Artificial Intelligence (ML & GA).
  • Develop a practical IoT-based healthcare monitoring system using ESP32 and biomedical sensors.
  • Implement real-time data transmission and visualization using Blynk & Firebase.

Chapter 1

Introduction & Background

Optical Sensors vs Conventional

Limitations of Conventional

  • Low sensitivity to small biological changes.
  • Bulky systems, increasing cost.
  • Affected by electromagnetic interference (EMI).
  • Limited real-time monitoring.

Optical Sensing Advantages

  • High sensitivity and fast response.
  • Immunity to EMI.
  • Label-free detection.
  • Enables advanced techniques like SPR and PCF.

Surface Plasmon Resonance (SPR)

SPR is an optical phenomenon at a metal–dielectric interface where incident light interacts with free electrons.

  • Extremely sensitive to Refractive Index (RI) variations near the metal surface.
  • Allows for real-time, label-free detection of chemical and biological substances.
  • The binding of an analyte modifies the local RI, resulting in a measurable shift in the resonance angle or wavelength.

Photonic Crystal Fiber (PCF)

PCFs are optical fibers with a periodic arrangement of air holes.

  • Precise control of light confinement and dispersion.
  • PCF-SPR sensors integrate PCF structures with SPR to achieve highly sensitive RI detection.
  • Engineered air-hole geometries enhance evanescent field interaction.

Chapter 3

Design, Simulation, and Performance Analysis

Proposed Sensor Geometry

  • Structure: 15 air holes arranged in a circular symmetric pattern.
  • Core: Solid silica core.
  • Plasmonic Layer: Thin gold (Au) layer deposited on the inner surface.
  • Analyte: Placed directly adjacent to the gold layer.

This circular geometry enhances the symmetry of the field distribution and increases interaction between light and the analyte compared to the original dual-core design.

Numerical Analysis (FEM)

Simulations performed using COMSOL Multiphysics with the Finite Element Method (FEM).

  • Confinement Loss: Calculated from the imaginary part of the effective refractive index.
  • Wavelength Sensitivity: Defined as the shift in resonance wavelength per RIU change.
    S_λ = Δλ_peak / Δn_a
  • Figure of Merit (FOM): Ratio of sensitivity to Full Width at Half Maximum (FWHM).

Phase Matching & Resonance

Resonance occurs when the propagation constant of the guided core mode matches the surface plasmon polariton (SPP) mode.

  • At resonance, optical power transfers from the core to the plasmonic mode.
  • This creates a sharp peak in confinement loss.
  • As analyte RI increases, the resonance peak shifts toward longer wavelengths (red shift).

Machine Learning Optimization

AI techniques were employed to bypass repeated, time-consuming FEM simulations.

  • Dataset: Generated by varying gold thickness, air-hole diameter, pitch, and analyte RI.
  • Model Performance: Achieved a coefficient of determination (R²) of 0.896.
  • Optimized Parameters: Gold Thickness = 40 nm, Air Hole Diameter = 850 nm, Pitch = 2 μm.

Chapter 4

Practical IoT Healthcare System

Why an IoT Alternative?

While PCF-SPR sensors offer extreme sensitivity, their practical implementation faces challenges:

  • Complex fabrication of photonic crystals.
  • High cost of optical components & spectrometers.
  • Requires specialized lab environments.

Solution: We developed a low-cost, real-time IoT healthcare monitoring system as a practical proof-of-concept.

Hardware Components

  • ESP32: Main microcontroller with built-in Wi-Fi.
  • MAX30102: Measures Heart Rate (BPM) and Blood Oxygen (SpO₂).
  • DHT11: Measures ambient temperature & humidity.
  • DS18B20: High-accuracy body temperature sensor.

Cloud Communication Architecture

  • Data Acquisition: Sensors read physiological data.
  • Processing: ESP32 digitizes and formats the data.
  • Cloud Database: Data is transmitted securely via Wi-Fi to Firebase Realtime Database.
  • Visualization: Displayed instantly on the Blynk mobile app and our custom web dashboard.
